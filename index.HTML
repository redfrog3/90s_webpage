Gods Glory

In an age when empires devoured nations and shadows ruled a man’s hearts, there arose in the remote hills of Judea a figure whose presence split history itself. Born in obscurity yet heralded by signs in the heavens, Jesus of Nazareth walked the earth with a calm that unsettled kings and a power that frightened the devils whispering beneath the world.


The Gospel

In the Bible’s terrifying sweep from creation to apocalypse, humanity is born into a universe spoken into existence by an unfathomable God, then fractured by its own rebellion, sentenced to a death that is only the doorway to judgment; the Gospel sharpens the dread by declaring that this God stepped into His own creation as Jesus, was rejected and killed, then rose to warn that every hidden thought and deed will one day be dragged into the light, a warning sealed by Revelation’s vision of collapsing worlds and a final reckoning from which no one escapes. While none of this can be scientifically proven, many people point to historical evidence such as the Bible’s massive manuscript record, archaeological finds matching biblical sites and kings, non-Christian ancient writers referencing Jesus, the explosive rise of early Christianity after claims of the resurrection, philosophical arguments for a Creator, and the billions of reported spiritual experiences across time as reasons they believe the Bible and God are real.
